# 31. Acquisition of Civilization Cards

**31.1** Each player may acquire one or more civilization cards by turning in commodity cards and treasury tokens and applying credits from previously purchased civilization cards. The face value is printed on each card.

**31.12** Civilization cards are acquired in **A.S.T. order**, allowing later players to see which cards others are buying first. Because there is no limit to the number of each type, this rule may be waived and all players may acquire simultaneously.

---

## 31.2 Purchase Cost

The cost must be met by a combination of:

### 31.3 Commodity Cards
Value is calculated per the set rules (28.51).

### 31.4 Tokens
Treasury tokens may be used, but a player **may not intentionally spend more treasury tokens than required**.

### 31.5 Credits

**31.51** Most civilization cards provide credits toward other cards of the same group (**group credits**) and **special credits** toward certain other cards.

**31.52** A summary of credits is on the Civilization Card Credits table.

**31.53** Credits may **not** be used in the same turn in which they are acquired. A player must wait until the **next turn** to use credits from newly acquired civilization cards. The simplest enforcement: players who purchase more than one card collect them all at the same time.

**31.54** A credit from an already-owned card may be applied toward **more than one** additional card in the same turn, but the credit may only be applied **once to each new card**.

> Example: A player holding Astronomy (20-point credit to all other sciences) may apply the 20-point credit to both Coinage and Medicine in the same turn, but may only apply it once to each.

**31.55** Four civilization cards belong to **two groups** each (Engineering, Mathematics, Literacy, Mysticism). They receive advantages of both groups.

**31.551** A dual-color card counts as **two groups** when determining epoch entry requirements (33.23).

**31.56** A player **must** use a credit if it applies to a card he is purchasing. He cannot choose to ignore the credit in order to spend treasury tokens.

**31.57** If a player has sufficient credits to acquire a card **at no cost**, he may do so.

**31.58** **No change is given** if the value of commodity cards and credits exceeds the cost. Any excess is lost.

---

## 31.6 Restrictions

**31.61** A player may acquire **one, and only one**, of each of the 24 civilization cards.

**31.62 Prerequisites:** Certain cards may only be acquired if another card was already acquired in a **previous turn**:
- **Engineering** required for: Road Building, Mining
- **Law** required for: Democracy, Philosophy
- **Enlightenment** required for: Monotheism, Theology

**31.63** Once acquired, civilization cards **may not be discarded or traded**.

---

## 31.7 Returning Excess Commodity Cards

**31.71** After completing purchases, players may retain **up to eight commodity cards** for the next turn. Players **may not conceal the number** of cards they retain. Excess cards of the player's choice must be surrendered, displayed, shuffled together with commodity cards used for purchases and calamities from the same turn, then placed face-down at the bottom of the appropriate trade card stack.

**31.72** Tradable calamity cards **may not** be retained or surrendered. If not traded and resolved against another player, they are resolved against the holder.

---

## 31.8 Restacking Returned Trade Cards

**31.81** Once all players have acquired civilization cards, all commodity cards used, excess commodity cards, and any tradable calamity cards drawn that turn are shuffled together and placed face-down at the bottom of the appropriate trade card stack.

**31.82** Non-tradable calamity cards drawn that turn are then placed at the bottom of the appropriate stack.

---

# 32. Civilization Card Attributes

**32.1** Details of how civilization cards affect calamities are found in the calamity rules. Credits towards additional cards are in the Civilization Card Credit table.

---

## 32.2 Crafts

### 32.21 Pottery (Craft — 45)
- Famine effect reduced by 4 unit points per Grain card held (30.312).
- Provides 10-point credit to all other Craft cards, Democracy, and Monotheism.

### 32.22 Cloth Making (Craft — 45)
- Ships may move into **one extra area** (five total) per phase.
- Provides 10-point credit to all other Craft cards, Democracy, and Monotheism.

### 32.23 Metalworking (Craft — 80)
- In conflicts, the holder removes tokens **after** all players without Metalworking.
- Provides 10-point credit to all other Craft cards, Democracy, Monotheism, and 20 points to Military.

### 32.24 Agriculture (Craft — 110)
- Population limit in areas occupied **solely** by this player's tokens is increased by **one**.
- Provides 10-point credit to all other Craft cards, Democracy, and Monotheism.

### 32.25 Road Building (Craft — 140)
- Holders may move tokens through one land area into a **second** land area per phase (first area must not contain enemy units, Barbarians, or Pirate cities; cannot be used to board ships).
- Aggravates Epidemic (+5 unit points), Civil Disorder (+1 city), and Iconoclasm and Heresy (+1 city).
- **Requires Engineering**.
- Provides 10-point credit to all other Craft cards, Democracy, and Monotheism.

### 32.26 Mining (Craft — 180)
- Allows increasing the value of any set of Iron, Bronze, Silver, Gems, or Gold by **one card** (once per turn). May not exceed the maximum printed on the card.
- Aggravates Slave Revolt (30.423).
- **Requires Engineering**.
- Provides 10-point credit to all other Craft cards, Democracy, and Monotheism.

---

## 32.3 Crafts/Sciences

### 32.31 Engineering (Craft/Science — 140)
- Attacker requires only **6 tokens** to attack a city (defender replaces with 5).
- Defender requires **8 tokens** to attack back (city replaced by 7 tokens).
- If both hold Engineering, effects **cancel**.
- Reduces effects of Earthquake and Flood.
- Required before Mining or Road Building.
- Provides 10-point credit to all other Craft cards, Democracy, Monotheism; 20 points to all other Science cards, Philosophy, and Theology.

---

## 32.4 Sciences

### 32.41 Astronomy (Science — 80)
- Ships may cross **open sea areas**.
- Provides 20-point credit to all other Science cards, Philosophy, Theology.

### 32.42 Coinage (Science — 110)
- Holder may vary taxation rate to 1 or 3 tokens per city (same rate for all cities that round).
- Provides 20-point credit to all other Science cards, Philosophy, Theology.

### 32.43 Medicine (Science — 140)
- Reduces Epidemic losses (30.613).
- Provides 20-point credit to all other Science cards, Philosophy, Theology.

---

## 32.5 Science/Arts

### 32.54 Mathematics (Science/Arts — 230)
- Provides 20-point credit to all other Science cards, 5 points to all other Arts cards and Law, 10 points to Enlightenment, and 25 points to Philosophy and Theology.

---

## 32.6 Arts

### 32.61 Drama and Poetry (Arts — 60)
- Reduces effects of Civil War (30.4122) and Civil Disorder (30.712).
- Provides credits: 5 to Mathematics, Music, Architecture, Law, Mysticism; 10 to Democracy, Enlightenment; 20 to Literacy.

### 32.62 Music (Arts — 60)
- Reduces effects of Civil War (30.4122) and Civil Disorder (30.712).
- Provides credits: 5 to Drama and Poetry, Architecture, Literacy, Law, Mysticism; 10 to Democracy, Enlightenment; 20 to Mathematics, Philosophy.

### 32.63 Architecture (Arts — 120)
- Holders may use treasury tokens to assist in building **one city per turn** (at least half must be on-board tokens; may not be used in areas with enemy units or Barbarians).
- Provides credits: 5 to all other Arts cards; 10 to Democracy, Enlightenment; 15 to Law.

---

## 32.7 Arts/Civics

### 32.71 Literacy (Arts/Civic — 110)
- Provides credits: 5 to all other Arts cards; 10 to Enlightenment; 25 to Law, Democracy, Philosophy.

---

## 32.8 Civics

### 32.81 Law (Civic — 170)
- Reduces effects of Civil Disorder (30.712) and Iconoclasm and Heresy (30.812).
- Required before Democracy or Philosophy.

### 32.82 Democracy (Civic — 200)
- Immune from tax revolts.
- Reduces effects of Civil War (30.4122) and Civil Disorder (30.712).
- **Requires Law**.

### 32.83 Military (Civic — 180)
- Players with Military always **move after** players without Military (similar effect on ship construction order).
- Civil War causes both factions to lose 5 unit points after resolution (30.414).
- Aggravates Civil Disorder (+1 city, 30.713).

### 32.84 Philosophy (Civic — 240)
- Alters Civil War faction composition (30.4124) and reduces Iconoclasm and Heresy (30.813, 30.819).
- **Requires Law**.

---

## 32.9 Religion

### 32.91 Mysticism (Religion/Arts — 50)
- Reduces Superstition effects (30.322).
- Provides credits: 5 to all other Arts cards; 15 to all other Religion cards.

### 32.92 Deism (Religion — 80)
- Reduces Superstition effects (30.322).
- Provides 15-point credit to Enlightenment, Monotheism, Theology.

### 32.93 Enlightenment (Religion — 150)
- Nullifies Superstition (30.322) and reduces Slave Revolt (30.423).
- Required before Monotheism or Theology.
- Provides 15-point credit to Monotheism and Theology.

### 32.94 Monotheism (Religion — 220)
- At the end of the calamity phase, the holder may **convert** occupants of any one area adjacent by land to an area containing his own units, replacing those units with his own. An area with a city is replaced by a city; an area with tokens is replaced by tokens.
- May not convert if insufficient units in stock. Target area must be land-adjacent to the player's units and may not be occupied by a player with Monotheism or Theology. Barbarian tokens and Pirate cities cannot be converted.
- Aggravates Iconoclasm and Heresy (+1 city, 30.815).
- **Requires Enlightenment**.

### 32.95 Theology (Religion — 250)
- Reduces Iconoclasm and Heresy effects (30.814, 30.819).
- A holder of Theology is **not affected by Monotheism**.
- **Requires Enlightenment**.
