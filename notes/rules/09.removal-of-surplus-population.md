# 26. Removal of Surplus Population

**26.1** After city construction, all surplus population is removed to stock:
- Areas **with cities** may not also contain tokens.
- Areas **without cities** may not contain more tokens than their population limit allows.

> **26.11 Agriculture:** The population limit in areas containing **only** tokens belonging to a player who holds Agriculture is increased by **one**. This effect is limited to areas that do not contain tokens belonging to other players. Agriculture has no effect in areas where tokens are co-existing or during conflict.

**26.2** Ships do **not** count towards population limits. Any number of ships may occupy the same area.

---

## 26.3 Checking for City Support

**26.31** After all surplus population is removed, each player checks for city support. Each player must have **two tokens on the board for every city in play**. Players who do not have enough tokens must reduce cities one at a time until the remaining cities are supported.

**26.32** Players reducing unsupported cities may select which cities to reduce, **subject to the requirement that newly constructed cities must be reduced before cities built or acquired in previous turns**.

---

## 26.4 City Reduction

**26.41** Cities are reduced by removing them from play and replacing them with the **maximum number of tokens** allowed by the area's population limit. These tokens can immediately be used as support for other vulnerable cities. If the player lacks sufficient stock tokens to meet the population limit, he replaces the city with whatever tokens he has. If other cities remain unsupported after this, they are **eliminated**.

**26.5** City support is checked at **only two points** each turn:
1. After removal of surplus population
2. After resolution of calamities

City support is not checked at any other time, except when resolving Slave Revolt (30.42).

**26.6** When city reduction is required as a result of calamities, the same procedure applies.
