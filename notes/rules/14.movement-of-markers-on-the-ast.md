# 33. Movement of Markers on the A.S.T.

**33.1** At the end of each turn, each player's marker moves **one space to the right** along the A.S.T. In some situations, a marker may not advance or may move **backward one space**.

**33.11** A player's marker may **not enter a new epoch** unless the epoch entry requirements are met.

---

## 33.2 Epoch Entry Requirements

| Epoch | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| **Stone Age** | None |
| **Early Bronze Age** | Two cities in play |
| **Late Bronze Age** | Three cities in play **and** ownership of at least **three groups** of civilization cards (three of the five colors present) |
| **Early Iron Age** | Four cities in play **and** ownership of at least **nine civilization cards** including cards from **all five groups** (all five colors present) |
| **Late Iron Age** | Five cities in play. Each space has a **point value** — to enter that space, the player's civilization card total must be **at least equal** to the printed value. Commodity cards and treasury are not counted. |

> Note: Some civilization cards belong to two groups and count as both (31.551).

---

## 33.3 Frozen Marker

If a player has fewer cities than the epoch entry requirement, his marker is **frozen** and may not advance further in that epoch until he has again constructed the required number of cities. This is determined at the time markers are moved.

---

## 33.4 Backward Movement

If a player ends a round with **no cities in play**, his marker moves **backward one space per turn**, unless the player is in the **Stone Age** (which has no city requirement).
