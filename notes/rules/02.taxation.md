# 19. Taxation

**19.1** Every player must transfer **two tokens from stock to treasury** for every city he has on the board. This is the **only** way tokens are moved into treasury, other than pillage (24.52).

**19.2** Players who hold **Coinage** may vary their taxation rate:
- Increase to **three tokens per city**, or
- Decrease to **one token per city**

The same tax rate must be levied on all cities in a given round. A rate may be set that causes some cities to revolt.

---

## 19.3 Revolts

**19.31** Revolts occur when a player does not have sufficient tokens in stock to pay required taxes. Once the shortfall is determined, the excess cities revolt. Revolts are resolved only after all other players have paid their taxes.

**19.32** The player with the **most units in stock** (cities = 5 each, tokens = 1 each) is the beneficiary of the revolt. He chooses which cities revolt and replaces them with his own cities. He may take over only as many cities as the original owner cannot pay for. If the beneficiary lacks enough cities in stock, the player with the next largest unit stock takes the remainder, and so on.

**19.33** In the rare case where no player can take over remaining revolting cities, they are **eliminated** instead.

**19.34** Cities belonging to players who hold **Democracy** never revolt.
