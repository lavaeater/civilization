# 28. Trade

**28.1** Players trade to build up sets of the same commodities, as sets are more valuable than individual cards. Trade is open to all players. Offers may be suspended, altered or withdrawn in open negotiation, but once trade cards have changed hands, **a deal is complete and cannot be revoked**.

**28.2** Trade involves only trade cards. Treasury tokens and civilization cards may **not** be included. Any one trade deal may involve **only two players**.

**28.3 Trade requirements:**
- Each trade must involve **at least three trade cards on each side**.
- A player with fewer than three trade cards **may not trade**.
- When negotiating, each player must **honestly** inform the other of:
  - The **number** of trade cards he wishes to trade
  - **At least two** of the trade cards involved

This information must be correct. The remaining card(s) need not be specified and may be any commodity or tradable calamity card, regardless of what was said. A player **may not show** his trade cards during negotiations, nor inform other players of trade details after completion.

> **Example:** A player announces "I want grain — I'll trade for grain." He agrees to trade three cards including two salt, for a grain, an iron, and two unknown cards. This guarantees the other player will receive two salt. The third card is unguaranteed and may be anything. Similarly, the announcing player can only be sure of receiving one grain and one iron; the other two cards could be anything.

**28.4** Trading continues until all players have completed all desired deals. A **time limit of no more than five minutes** is strongly recommended.

## 28.5 Commodity Card Sets

**28.51** When a player holds more than one commodity card of the same type, the combination's value is:

> **value = (number of cards)² × (card's face value)**

Example: three Grain cards (face value 4) = 3 × 3 × 4 = **36** (vs. 4 + 4 + 4 = 12).

**28.52** Set values are printed on each commodity card. Different commodities, even of the same face value, may **not** be combined into sets.

> **28.53 Mining:** Mining increases the value of any set of Iron, Bronze, Silver, Gems, or Gold by **one card** (calculated as if one additional card of that type is held). This effect may only be used **once per turn**. Mining may not increase a set's value beyond the maximum printed on the commodity card.
>
> Example: Three Iron cards with Mining are valued as four Iron cards (32 instead of 18). Mining cannot be used for both Iron and Gems in the same turn.
