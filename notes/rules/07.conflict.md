# 24. Conflict

**24.1** Conflict occurs when tokens of two or more nations occupy the same area **and** the total tokens exceed the area's population limit. An area containing a city is considered **fully populated**. Co-existence without conflict is possible if the population limit is not exceeded.

**24.11**
- Conflicts may occur between tokens, between tokens and cities, and between tokens and cities defended by tokens.
- All conflicts occur in **land areas**.
- Ships are **never** involved in conflicts, even if they transported tokens to battle. Ships cannot be captured or eliminated.

**24.12** Tokens and cities eliminated by conflict are **returned to stock**.

---

## 24.2 Conflict Between Tokens

**24.21** Players remove one token at a time alternately until only one player's tokens remain in the area **or** the population level is no longer exceeded. A conflict may end in co-existence. The player with the **fewest** tokens removes first.

**24.22** If both players have equal tokens, they remove simultaneously. An area that can support only one token will thus end up depopulated.

**24.23** If more than two players are involved, tokens are removed in **ascending order of strength**. Two or more players may be required to remove tokens simultaneously.

> **24.24 Metalworking:** Players who hold Metalworking remove their tokens **after** players who do not hold Metalworking, regardless of relative token counts. Conflict between two Metalworking holders is resolved normally.

---

## 24.3 Conflicts Between Tokens and Cities

**24.31** Cities remain standing unless attacked by **seven or more tokens** belonging to the same nation. Fewer than seven attacking tokens are simply removed without affecting the city.

**24.32** If a city is attacked by the required number of tokens, the defender **replaces the city with six tokens** and the resulting conflict is resolved. If the defending player has fewer than six tokens in stock, he replaces the city with what he has.

> Players are entitled to resolve any other conflicts involving their tokens before resolving attacks on their cities, so cities may be replaced by the maximum number of tokens.

**24.33** Two or more players may **not combine** to attack a city. If two players have tokens in an area containing another player's city, they battle among themselves until only one player's tokens remain; that survivor may then attack the city if sufficient tokens survive.

**24.34** When a Pirate city is attacked, it is replaced by unused tokens solely for combat. Any surviving pirate tokens are removed from the board.

> **24.35 Engineering:**
> - A player who holds Engineering requires only **six tokens** to attack a city. The defending city is replaced by **five tokens**.
> - **Eight tokens** are required to attack a city belonging to a player who holds Engineering. The city is replaced by **seven tokens**.
> - If **both** the attacker and defender hold Engineering, the effects **cancel**.

---

## 24.4 Conflicts Between Tokens and Cities Defended by Tokens

**24.41** Conflict between tokens is resolved **before** attacks on cities. Tokens defending a city must be eliminated before the city is attacked. The city may only be attacked if sufficient attacking tokens survive.

---

## 24.5 Consequences of City Elimination

**24.51 Drawing a trade card:** When a player attacks and eliminates a city, the attacking player **immediately draws, at random, one of the victim's trade cards** and retains it. If the victim has no trade cards, no draw occurs.

**24.52 Pillage:** In addition to drawing a trade card, the successful attacker may transfer **up to three tokens from stock to treasury** to reflect pillage. The attacker may choose to transfer fewer than three. The number pillaged may not exceed tokens in attacker's stock and never exceeds three per city. The victim's treasury and stock are unaffected.

**24.53** No trade card is drawn and no pillage occurs when a city is:
- Converted by Monotheism
- Captured by Pirates
- Eliminated by Barbarians
- Lost as a result of any other calamity
- Lost to a tax revolt

A trade card **is** drawn and pillage **does** occur when one player eliminates another player's city by **direct attack**. A player who attacks and eliminates a Pirate city may pillage it.
