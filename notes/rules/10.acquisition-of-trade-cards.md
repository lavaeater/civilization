# 27. Acquisition of Trade Cards

**27.1** Players draw **one trade card from as many trade card stacks as they have cities** on the board. The player with the **fewest cities** draws first, followed by players with progressively more cities.

**27.2** Cards are always collected the same way: draw one card from each stack in order starting from stack 1, until the player has drawn from as many stacks as he has cities. A player with **three cities** draws from stacks 1, 2, and 3 only — he cannot draw from stack 4 or higher until he has more than three cities.

**27.21** If a stack is **empty**, the player simply foregoes drawing from that stack. He may not replace the lost card from another stack.

**27.3** Players **may not disclose** what trade cards they have drawn, as they may have acquired a tradable calamity to trade away. Non-tradable calamities (Volcano/Earthquake, Famine, Civil War, Flood) must be **retained until all trading is complete**.

**27.4** Trade cards must be **held in secret**. While in a player's hand, trade cards are the only item not open to inspection by other players.

---

## 27.5 Buying Gold, Ivory or Piracy

**27.51** Any player, regardless of city count, may buy **one or more trade cards from the ninth (Gold/Ivory/Piracy) stack** at a cost of **18 tokens from treasury per card**. Spent tokens are returned to stock.

Trade cards are purchased from the ninth stack **immediately after the purchasing player collects his trade cards**, before any other players collect theirs.
