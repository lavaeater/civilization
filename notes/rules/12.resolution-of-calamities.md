# 29. Resolution of Calamities

**29.1** All calamities are resolved immediately after trading ends, before the acquisition of civilization cards.

**29.2** Non-tradable calamity cards are retained by the drawing player until the end of trading. They are then resolved against the drawing player.

**29.3** Tradable calamity cards are not revealed when drawn. A player who draws a tradable calamity may trade it away. The recipient may also trade it, and so on — there is **no limit** to the number of times a tradable calamity may change hands. At the end of trading, all calamity cards are revealed by whoever holds them.

**29.4** A player who drew a tradable calamity need not trade it, but **calamity cards cannot be held for future turns**. All calamities take effect on the turn in which they are drawn. A player who retains a tradable calamity becomes the primary victim. If the retained calamity calls for secondary victims (Epidemic, Iconoclasm and Heresy, Piracy), any other eligible player may be named as secondary.

**29.5** No player may be the primary victim of **more than two calamities** in the same turn. If a player receives more than two, the calamities are shuffled and two are drawn at random. The rest are returned to the appropriate trade card stacks. There is **no restriction** on the infliction of secondary effects.

**29.6** Calamities are resolved in **ascending order** (Volcanic Eruption/Earthquake first, Piracy last). Non-tradable calamities are resolved before tradable calamities of the same numeric value (e.g., Volcanic Eruption before Treachery, Famine before Superstition, etc.).

**29.61** The player holding a calamity card at the end of trading is the **primary victim**. The player who traded a tradable calamity to the primary victim **may not be selected as a secondary victim**.

**29.62** When resolving calamities, cities are worth up to five unit points and tokens are worth one unit point. If a city is **reduced**, the surviving replacement tokens are **not counted** towards the calamity.

**29.63** Players must fulfill calamity losses by the **exact amount required**. If unable, they may exceed the amount, but only by as small an amount as necessary.

**29.64** Whenever a calamity requires the primary victim to direct effects against other players, the primary victim **must** do so.

**29.65** Barbarian tokens and Pirate cities are **never** affected by calamities.

**29.7** After all calamities are resolved, they are set aside. After civilization card purchases, tradable calamity cards are shuffled with returned trade cards of the same value and placed face-down at the **bottom** of the appropriate stack. Non-tradable calamity cards are then placed at the bottom of their stack.

**29.8** Once all calamities are resolved, players must again **check for city support** (26.3).

# 30. Calamities

**30.1** Calamities are listed in resolution order. These effects are also on the Summary of Calamity Effects sheet.

---

## 30.2 Second Level Calamities

### 30.21 Volcanic Eruption or Earthquake

**30.211** If the primary victim has any cities in an area touched by a volcano, the **volcano erupts** and eliminates **all units, regardless of ownership**, in all areas touched by that volcano. If the victim has cities touching more than one volcano, the eruption occurs at the site causing the **greatest total damage** to the primary victim and any secondary victims. On a tie, the primary victim chooses.

**30.212** If the primary victim has **no cities** in any volcano area, one of his cities is destroyed by **earthquake**. One city belonging to another player is reduced. This second city must be in an area adjacent to the destroyed city, even across water. The site is chosen to cause greatest total damage.

> **30.213 Engineering:** An earthquake **reduces** rather than destroys the primary victim's city. A player who holds Engineering may **not** be selected as a secondary victim of Earthquake. Engineering has **no effect** on Volcanoes.

---

### 30.22 Treachery

**30.221** One city belonging to the primary victim is **replaced by one city belonging to the player who traded the card**. If the trader has no available cities, the victim's city is eliminated. The trader selects the city.

**30.222** If Treachery is drawn and **not traded**, one city belonging to that player is **reduced**. Since no one traded the card, no other player benefits.

---

## 30.3 Third Level Calamities

### 30.31 Famine

**30.311** The primary victim loses **10 unit points**. The primary victim must also instruct other players to remove **20 unit points total**, with **no more than 8** from any one player. The primary victim decides the allocation; the secondary victims choose which units to remove.

> **30.312 Pottery:** Players who hold Pottery may reduce their losses by **4 unit points for each Grain trade card held**. Grain cards used for this purpose are **not discarded** but must be placed **face up** in front of the player until the end of the turn and **may not be used to acquire civilization cards** until the following turn.

---

### 30.32 Superstition

**30.321** Three cities belonging to the primary victim are **reduced**. The primary victim chooses which cities.

> **30.322 Religion cards** (effects are **not cumulative** — the highest level Religion card governs):
> - **Mysticism**: two cities reduced instead of three
> - **Deism**: one city reduced
> - **Enlightenment**: no effect

---

## 30.4 Fourth Level Calamities

### 30.41 Civil War

**30.411** The primary victim's nation is divided into two factions. The player with the **most unit points in stock** (tokens = 1, cities = 5) is the beneficiary. If the primary victim has the most units in stock, **there is no Civil War**.

**30.412 Faction composition:**

**30.4121** The primary victim begins by selecting **15 unit points** of his own units for the first faction.

> **30.4122** Additional unit points selected by the primary victim:
> - Holds **Music**: +5 unit points
> - Holds **Drama and Poetry**: +5 unit points
> - Holds **Democracy**: +10 unit points
> *(These effects are cumulative)*

**30.4123** After the primary victim completes his selection, the beneficiary selects an additional **20 unit points** belonging to the primary victim to complete the first faction.

> **30.4124 Philosophy:** The first faction is automatically comprised of **15 units chosen by the beneficiary**, regardless of any other civilization cards held by the primary victim.

**30.413** Whatever remains constitutes the **second faction**. If there is no second faction, there is no Civil War.

> **30.414 Military:** Five unit points are removed from **each faction** to reflect increased destructiveness. Units are removed after factions are selected. Each player must, if possible, remove units from areas adjacent to the other faction.

**30.415** The primary victim chooses whether to continue with the **first or second faction**. The primary victim retains his stock, ships, treasury, civilization cards, and A.S.T. position. The beneficiary annexes whichever faction the victim does not retain by replacing units with his own. If he runs out of units, the next player with the most units in stock takes over the remainder.

---

### 30.42 Slave Revolt

**30.421** **Fifteen tokens** belonging to the primary victim may not be used to support his cities. This effect is resolved immediately. After the calamity phase ends, tokens function normally.

**30.422** Cities are reduced one at a time; newly available replacement tokens may support remaining cities. If the victim has fewer than 15 tokens on the board, only those tokens are affected. Tokens placed on the board after resulting reductions may always be used for city support.

> **30.423 Modifiers:**
> - **Mining**: an additional **5 tokens** may not be used for city support
> - **Enlightenment**: the number of tokens that may not support is reduced by **5**
> - If a player holds **both Mining and Enlightenment**, the effects **cancel**

---

## 30.5 Fifth Level Calamities

### 30.51 Flood

**30.511** If the primary victim has units on a flood plain (4.42), he loses a **maximum of 17 unit points** from that flood plain. Cities are vulnerable if built in areas with **no city site or a white (flood plain) city site**. Cities on **black city sites** are safe.

**30.512 Secondary victims:** 10 unit points of units on the **same flood plain** belonging to one or more secondary victims are also removed. The primary victim divides the 10-point loss among secondary victims; the victims themselves choose which units to lose. If total other units on the flood plain total 10 or fewer, **all those players are automatically secondary victims** and all their units are eliminated.

**30.513** If the primary victim has units on more than one flood plain, the flood occurs on the flood plain with the **greatest number of his unit points**. Tie: primary victim chooses.

**30.514** If the primary victim has **no units on any flood plain**, one of his coastal cities is **eliminated**. The primary victim chooses. If the victim has no coastal cities, he is **unaffected**.

> **30.515 Engineering:**
> - A primary or secondary victim who holds Engineering loses a **maximum of 7 unit points** from flood (instead of 17/10).
> - If a primary victim who holds Engineering has **no units on a flood plain**, one of his coastal cities is **reduced** rather than eliminated.

---

### 30.52 Barbarian Hordes

**30.521 Initial placement:**

**30.5211** Fifteen tokens belonging to **an unused nation** are placed in one of the primary victim's **start areas** — the one causing the **greatest damage**. If the victim has no units in any start area, Barbarians are placed in an empty start area. They may only enter an area with other nations' units if no other option exists.

**30.5212** Immediately after placement, conflict is resolved between Barbarians and any units in that area.

**30.523 Continued movement:**

**30.5231** Once initial conflict is resolved, surplus Barbarians (beyond the area's population limit) move to the **adjacent area causing the greatest damage to the primary victim**. Conflict is resolved again.

**30.5232** This repeats until no surplus Barbarian tokens remain. At the end of the calamity phase, surviving Barbarians may not exceed population limits.

**30.5233** Barbarians always move as a unit (except when leaving tokens in already-occupied areas). They may move across water boundaries but **not across open sea areas**.

**30.5234** Barbarians always move into the area causing the **greatest immediate damage** to the primary victim. No calculation is made for overall strategic damage — the determination is made for each movement in turn.

**30.5235** All Barbarian movement and conflict is completed **before** any other calamities are resolved. Once stopped, Barbarians remain until eliminated. They do not increase their population and may **not** be selected as secondary calamity victims.

**30.524 Conflict with other nations:** Barbarians must enter areas occupied solely by the primary victim's cities or tokens, provided they can inflict damage. If unable, they may enter empty areas or areas with other nations to reach the nearest vulnerable primary victim units.

**30.5242** If Barbarians enter areas with other nations, they engage in conflict with those units. Barbarians do not attack their own tokens.

**30.525 Resolving ties:** The player who traded the calamity to the primary victim selects which area Barbarians enter when there is a tie. If not traded, the player with the most units in stock decides.

**30.526** If Barbarians eliminate a city, **no trade card is drawn** from the victim.

**30.527** **Crete** may **not** be the primary victim of Barbarian Hordes.

---

## 30.6 Sixth Level Calamities

### 30.61 Epidemic

**30.611** The primary victim loses **16 unit points**. The primary victim must also order other players to lose **25 unit points total**, with **no more than 10** from any one player. The player who traded Epidemic to the primary victim **may not be selected as a secondary victim**.

**30.612** A nation removing tokens due to Epidemic must **leave at least one token** in each affected area. Cities eliminated by Epidemic are replaced by at least one token; thus cities account for a **maximum of 4 unit points** when calculating Epidemic losses.

> **30.613 Medicine:**
> - Primary victim: losses reduced by **8 unit points**
> - Secondary victim: losses reduced by **5 unit points**

> **30.614 Road Building:** A player who holds Road Building loses an **additional 5 unit points** from Epidemic, both as primary and secondary victim.

**30.615** The effects of 30.613–30.614 are **cumulative**.

---

## 30.7 Seventh Level Calamities

### 30.71 Civil Disorder

**30.711** All but **three** of the primary victim's cities are **reduced**. The primary victim chooses which cities.

> **30.712** The number of cities reduced is **decreased by one** for each of the following held:
> - Music
> - Drama and Poetry
> - Law
> - Democracy

> **30.713 Military**: The number of cities reduced is **increased by one**.

> **30.714 Road Building**: The number of cities reduced is **increased by one**.

**30.715** The effects of 30.712–30.714 are **cumulative**.

---

## 30.8 Eighth Level Calamities

### 30.81 Iconoclasm and Heresy

**30.811** **Four** of the primary victim's cities are **reduced**. The primary victim chooses which cities.

> **30.812 Law**: cities reduced decreased by **1**
> **30.813 Philosophy**: cities reduced decreased by **1**
> **30.814 Theology**: cities reduced decreased by **3**
> **30.815 Monotheism**: cities reduced increased by **1**
> **30.816 Road Building**: cities reduced increased by **1**

**30.817** The effects of 30.812–30.816 are **cumulative**.

**30.818** The primary victim must also order the reduction of **two cities belonging to other players**. The player who traded Iconoclasm and Heresy may not be selected as a secondary victim.

> **30.819 Secondary victim protections:**
> - A secondary victim who holds **Philosophy** may not lose more than **one city**.
> - A player who holds **Theology** **cannot be named** as a secondary victim.

---

## 30.9 Ninth Level Calamities

### 30.91 Piracy

**30.911** The primary victim loses **two coastal cities**. The **trading player selects** the cities. These cities are replaced by **two Pirate cities** (using the unused nation's tokens).

**30.912** Two coastal cities belonging to **two other players** are similarly replaced by Pirate cities, even if the primary victim had fewer than two coastal cities. The **primary victim selects** the secondary victims. Secondary victims may each lose only **one city**. The player who traded Piracy may not be a secondary victim.

**30.913** Pirate cities do **not require city support** and remain until attacked and destroyed. When attacked, a Pirate city is replaced by unused tokens for combat; surviving pirate tokens are then eliminated. When a pirate city is destroyed, the attacker **may pillage** it.
