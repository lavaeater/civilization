# 23. Movement

**23.1** Once ship building is completed, players may move some, all, or none of their tokens and ships. Cities may **not** be moved. A player may **not** move another player's units.

**23.2** Players move in **census order** (most tokens first).

> **23.21 Military:** Players who hold Military always move **after** players who do not hold Military. Order between Military holders is determined normally by census order.

> **23.22 Barbarians** move only during the calamity phase in which they appear.

---

## 23.3 Token Movement

Tokens may be moved either:
- Across **one land boundary** into an adjacent land area, or
- **Onto a ship** currently occupying the same area.

Tokens may **not** move across both land and water in the same turn.

> **23.31 Road Building:** Players who hold Road Building may move their tokens through one land area into a **second** land area in the same movement phase. The first area entered may not contain units belonging to another player, Barbarians, or a Pirate city. Road Building may **not** be used to move tokens through a land area and then board a ship.

**23.4** Any number of tokens belonging to any number of players may be moved into the same area. Tokens may be moved into an area containing a city. Tokens may exceed an area's population limit, although this may result in later elimination.

---

## 23.5 Movement of Ships

**23.51** A ship may carry **up to five tokens** at one time. Only tokens that have **not been moved overland** in the current movement phase may embark onto and move with a ship.

**23.52** Ships may only move across **water boundaries**. A ship may move into up to **four water areas** per movement phase. Ships may **not** move into open sea areas unless the player holds Astronomy. Ships may travel in any water area including lakes, but may not cross all-land boundaries.

> **23.53 Cloth Making:** Ships may move into **one extra area** (five total) per movement phase.

> **23.54 Astronomy:** Ships may move into **open sea areas**.

**23.55** Ships may end movement in any reachable water area other than an open sea area, regardless of whose ships or tokens occupy it.

**23.56 Embarkation/Debarkation:**
- A ship may participate in any number of embarkations and debarkations during the same phase, and may retrace its route within the area limit.
- A ship may ferry two loads of tokens across a narrow strait.
- Tokens must be embarked and debarked during the same movement phase — **a token may not remain aboard a ship at the end of a phase**.
- A token may **not** travel on more than one ship in the same movement phase.

**23.57** In Greece, some areas have two distinct coastlines. Ships may enter from either side but must leave from the **same side**. Ships may not cross directly from one coastline to another.
